What is a derivative?

  • An easy way to think of a derivative is the change of rate. For example, velocity is the change of position over time. Velocity is also the derivative of position. Look at this graph below.
  • To find the velocity during a time interval you need to calculate the slope. Take the change of position and divide it by the change of time. From 0 to 1 seconds, the position remains at 20 meters. The average velocity during this interval is 0 m/s.
  • From 1 to 3 seconds, the position increases from 20 meters to 60 meters. What is the average velocity during this period?

    • 20 m/s

    Chain Rule

    • Tip: to take the derivative of a term like 5x2. First, mulitply the coeffecient by the exponent (the exponent is 2 and the coeffecient is 5). Then, subtract the exponent by 1.
      • f (x) = Cxn   f '(x) = (Cn)xn-1
    • Example 1
      • f (x) = x2
      • f '(x) = 2x
    • Example 2
      • f (x) = 2x3
      • f '(x) = 6x2
